SELECTED WORK

Karban Airzone

A ceiling mounted smart airflow system combining a fan, air purifier and light. I led the end to end product experience for its second version.

Role Lead Designer
Product App UI and Remote
Timeline 9 months
Domain Hardware and Software

The problem

Karban's first version crossed 1 crore in revenue and raised 8.93 crore in early stage funding. For version two, the brief was to redesign the full user experience. The hardware worked, but most users only used it as a regular fan. The air purification, the light, all the other features went completely unnoticed.

What the research showed

57 responses (survey)
31 reviews (product)
200+ calls (support)

Three problems kept coming up. Users had no way to know if the air purifier was actually working. There was nothing interactive to engage with, so the remote just sat in a drawer. And when anything went wrong, people called support because the product did not explain itself.

Three things to fix

  • 01 Making AQI visible without adding clutter
  • 02 Giving users a reason to interact with the device
  • 03 Letting the product communicate on its own

What we shipped

A redesigned app and remote for version two. The app shows real time air quality, gives users precise control over each device feature, and sends proactive notifications so they never have to wonder what is happening. The remote got clearer labelling, better hierarchy, and actually gives feedback about device status. I also worked with engineering to keep cloud costs in check and collaborated with marketing to reposition the product as a smart airflow system rather than a premium fan.
